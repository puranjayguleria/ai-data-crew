WhatsApp Marketing Guide for Local Businesses

Price: ₹99

1. Set up a clean business profile
Use a recognizable business name, logo, business category, short description, working hours, and quick contact options. Customers decide trust in seconds.

2. Create 3 message buckets
Do not send random promotions. Use:
- Welcome messages
- Offer messages
- Reminder / follow-up messages

3. Build consent first
Only message people who opted in via store visit, website form, Instagram DM, or QR code. Consent improves response rate and keeps communication ethical.

4. Use a simple weekly schedule
Monday: useful tip
Wednesday: product/service highlight
Friday: limited-time offer or reminder

5. Keep messages short
Best format:
- One-line hook
- One benefit
- One CTA
Example: ‘This week only: free skin consultation with any facial booking. Reply BOOK and we’ll reserve your slot.’

6. Use catalog-style selling
For service businesses, create mini menus in text:
- Starter package
- Popular package
- Premium package
This reduces back-and-forth and speeds up buying.

7. Follow up without spamming
If no reply, send one reminder after 48 hours. Do not send daily nudges.

8. Use status updates
Post testimonials, before/after results, limited offers, and FAQs in WhatsApp Status so warm leads see proof without direct pressure.

9. Track 4 numbers
- Messages sent
- Replies received
- Bookings/sales
- Repeat customers

10. Ready-to-use templates
Template 1 — Welcome:
Hi [Name], thanks for contacting [Business]. Here’s our current service list and this week’s best offer. Reply with your goal and I’ll recommend the best option.

Template 2 — Offer:
Hi [Name], we’re running a 3-day offer on [service/product]. You get [benefit]. Want details?

Template 3 — Reminder:
Just a quick reminder that the [offer] ends tomorrow. If you want me to reserve it for you, reply YES.

Template 4 — Review request:
Thanks for choosing us, [Name]. If you liked the experience, reply with a quick review — it helps a lot.

Template 5 — Repeat sale:
It’s been a while since your last order/visit. We’ve got a simple offer for returning customers this week: [offer]. Want it?
