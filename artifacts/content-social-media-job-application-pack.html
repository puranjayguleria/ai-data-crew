Sample 1: LinkedIn post for a B2B founder

Hook: Most B2B content fails because it tries to sound smart instead of being useful.

If your audience has to reread your post to understand it, you’ve already lost them. Strong B2B content does 3 things well: it names a real problem, gives one practical insight, and ends with a clear next step. Simplicity wins attention. Specificity wins trust. Consistency wins pipeline.

CTA: Want better inbound from LinkedIn? Start by turning one client question into one post every day for 10 days.

Sample 2: Instagram caption for a local business Reel

Hook: Most local businesses don’t need more posts. They need better hooks.

If your Reel doesn’t answer “why should I care?” in the first 2 seconds, people scroll. Try this structure: 1) call out a common mistake, 2) show the fix quickly, 3) end with one action step. That format is easier to save, share, and remember. Save this before your next post if you want content that actually brings inquiries.

Sample 3: Short blog sample

Title: Why Consistent Content Beats Occasional Viral Posts

Many brands overvalue virality and undervalue consistency. A viral post can create a spike in attention, but a consistent publishing rhythm builds familiarity and trust. For service businesses, trust is what converts. A practical content system—two educational posts, one proof post, and one CTA post each week—often outperforms random bursts of activity. The goal is not to impress everyone. The goal is to stay relevant to the right people long enough that they remember you when they need help.
