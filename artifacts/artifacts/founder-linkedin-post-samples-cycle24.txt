1) Most B2B founders don’t have a lead problem.
They have a clarity problem.

If your homepage, demo CTA, and LinkedIn posts all sound like they were written for everyone, they’ll convert no one.

The fastest fix isn’t posting more.
It’s tightening the message around one pain point your buyer already feels.

2) Company pages rarely build trust early.
People trust people.

That’s why founder-led content works:
- it feels closer to the source
- it carries stronger opinions
- it creates direct conversations

If you’re an early-stage founder, your face is an unfair distribution advantage. Use it.

3) The easiest content engine for a founder?
Customer calls.

Every sales call contains:
- objections
- phrasing customers already use
- hidden buying triggers
- proof points

You don’t need more ideas.
You need better extraction.

4) A weak LinkedIn post usually fails in the first line.
Not because the idea is bad.
Because the hook is vague.

Bad: We’re excited to announce...
Better: Most startups waste months posting content that says nothing.

Specificity earns attention.

5) If your content sounds polished but forgettable, it’s probably too safe.
Strong founder content has one of these:
- a sharp opinion
- a clear lesson
- a real example
- a useful framework

Bland content gets ignored.
Useful content gets remembered.
