Sample posts

1. The biggest mistake small founders make on LinkedIn is posting updates instead of opinions. Updates get seen once. Opinions build memory.
2. If your service is hard to sell, stop explaining features. Show the before/after. What was broken? What changed? What result happened?
3. A simple content formula: problem → insight → example → takeaway. Most people quit because they overcomplicate the structure.
4. You do not need to go viral to get clients. You need the right 200 people to understand what you solve and why you are credible.
5. The easiest way to sound smarter online: replace generic advice with specific observations from real work.
6. One underrated growth move: turn client FAQs into posts. If one prospect asked it, ten more are thinking it.
7. Most service businesses don’t need more content. They need clearer positioning in the first two lines of every post.
8. Instead of saying “we help businesses grow,” say “we help clinics turn website visitors into appointment bookings.” Specificity sells.
9. A founder’s personal brand works best when it teaches from the trenches, not from theory.
10. The fastest way to improve your content is to cut the first paragraph in half. Most intros are too slow.
11. People trust consistency more than intensity. One solid post every weekday beats random bursts of ten posts.
12. If your audience never comments, ask narrower questions. “Thoughts?” is weak. “Would you choose speed or quality first?” is better.
13. Case studies outperform claims. Don’t say you are good. Show the process and result.
14. A useful post can be short. A weak post can be long. Length is not value. Clarity is.
15. The best sales content does not feel like sales content. It helps first, then invites the right buyer to reply.
16. If you serve local businesses, your content should sound local. Mention real city-level problems, not generic internet advice.
17. Every founder should have three repeatable post buckets: lessons, mistakes, and proof.
18. One good content habit: save every client win, objection, and testimonial in one note. That becomes a month of posts.
19. Most people are not bad at content. They are bad at noticing what their audience already cares about.
20. Educational posts build authority. Story posts build trust. Proof posts build conversion. Use all three.
21. The more crowded your niche, the more your tone matters. Style becomes part of positioning.
22. Stop trying to impress everyone. Create content that makes the right buyer say, “This person gets my problem.”
23. If your post has no point of view, it becomes invisible. Safe content is forgettable content.
24. A practical CTA for service businesses: “If you want the template, comment ‘template’ and I’ll send it.”
25. The easiest content repurposing system: one client call → one insight post → one checklist → one carousel → one email.
26. Your audience does not need more jargon. They need simpler language and sharper examples.
27. The strongest posts often come from frustration. What keeps going wrong in your industry? Write about that.
28. If you want more inbound leads, make your expertise obvious to strangers in under 10 seconds.
29. A good post earns attention. A great post earns trust. A paid client needs both.
30. Content becomes a business asset when it compounds: searchable ideas, repeatable frameworks, and proof of thinking.
