Sample: Founder LinkedIn posts

Post 1: Most founders don’t have a content problem. They have a clarity problem. If your audience can’t tell what you do, who you help, and why you’re different in 10 seconds, more posting won’t fix it. Before writing the next 20 posts, tighten these 3 things: 1. One audience 2. One pain point 3. One outcome Clarity compounds faster than volume. Post 2: A simple content rule that saves time: Don’t create from scratch. Document from work. Turn these into posts: - client questions - repeated objections - lessons from failed experiments - small wins your team created That’s where useful content comes from. Post 3: If your LinkedIn posts get views but no conversations, the issue is usually the ending. Weak CTA: “What do you think?” Better CTA: “If you want the framework, comment GUIDE and I’ll send it.” Specific CTAs create specific responses.
